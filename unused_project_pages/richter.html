San Andreas Fault

Study of Spatial Distribution of Earthquakes

To identify earthquakes caused by the San Andreas Fault Line from 1900-2000 within the state boundary of California, was the research goal for our Spatial Databases project. My partner and I used spatial analysis techniques and geoprocessing tools to identify the earthquakes that fall within the San Andreas Fault Line damage buffer zone. We used CartoDB to illustrate the earthquake data by different attributes in a variety of visualizations including an intensity map, a cluster map, and a time chloropleth map.

A categorical map that is featured in the image illustrates the earthquakes location and their intensity based on the Richter magnitude scale. The second image shows the extent of the San Andreas Fault in orange.

Software: PostgresSQL, Quantum GIS, ArcGIS, CartoDB, and Python
