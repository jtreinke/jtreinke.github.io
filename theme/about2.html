Welcome! My name is Jennifer Reinke and I am pursuing my master's degree in the Geographic Information Science (GIS) program and minoring in Design at the University of Minnesota - Twin Cities. I will graduate in December 2015. Prior, I achieved my Bachelor of Arts from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where I majored in Geography and French with a minor in European Studies.

Currently, I am a Graduate Research Assistant at the John R. Borchert Map Library through U-Spatial, the university's consortium for spatial research. I work on an array of projects that help establish spatial data to an online platform. For illustration, I authored FGDC compliant metadata using Arc Catalog for XML manipulation including the Minnesota Geological Survey's data records accessible at the UMNs Open Geoportal. I also develop methodology for digitizing for historical aerial photograph indices.

GIS applications first sparked my interest during my undergraduate studies when I was fortunate enough to take cartography. I found myself immersed in a challenging course where questions concerning the significance of color and design, and the emphasis of psychology behind composition were being raised. Mapping continued to draw my attention as I lived and worked in Europe after graduating from university. Navigating with maps was often a factor in surviving daily life, which I witnessed as well while working in the natural resources field. It was the value of visual communication that enchanted my creative side especially within a spatial context due to the complexity of depicting geographical information.

Nonverbal communication is the most effective way to transcend information. I want to utilize design concepts to enhance user-experience for web development, cartography, and graphic design, as vehicle to transfer ideas and encourage discussion.
