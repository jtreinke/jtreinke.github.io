Mapping Balzac's Paris

Explored the Social Topography of 19th Century Paris

Through my graduate assistant position at the John R. Borchert Map Library, I was responsible for mapping geographic sites in Paris, referenced in three Balzac novels to detect patterns and decipher the subtext of the chosen localities. I datamined French versions of The Magic Skin, Father Goriot,and Lost Illusions, to identify geographic plot points and proceeded to geocode the place names and georeference a digital copy of an 1814 map of Paris.

I created an interactive web map to visualize key elements regarding the social topography of Balzacâ€™s Paris, to assist students with understanding French literature as well as this historical period beyond what they might grasp through text analysis alone.
