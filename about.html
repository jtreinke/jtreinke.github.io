Welcome! My name is Jennifer Reinke and I am a recent graduate of the Master of Geographic Information Science (GIS) with a Design minor from the University of Minnesota - Twin Cities. I achieved my Bachelor of Arts from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where I majored in Geography and French with a minor in European Studies.

Currently, I am a Graduate Research Assistant at the John R. Borchert Map Library through U-Spatial, the university's consortium for spatial research. I worked on an array of projects ranging from digital humnaities, web development, ..... I developed GIS skills in various disciplines such as working in the field with the Natural Resources Department for the City of Lakeville, remote sensing projects for the Polar Geospatial Center, and currently metadata curation, web development, and historical aerial photograph manipulation as a graduate assistant for the John R. For illustration, I authored FGDC compliant metadata using Arc Catalog for XML manipulation including the Minnesota Geological Survey's data records accessible at the UMNs Open Geoportal.

GIS applications first sparked my interest during my undergraduate studies when I was fortunate enough to take cartography. I found myself immersed in a challenging course where questions concerning the significance of color and design, and the emphasis of psychology behind composition were being raised. Mapping continued to draw my attention as I lived and worked in Europe after graduating from university. Navigating with maps was often a factor in surviving daily life, which I witnessed as well while working in the natural resources field. It was the value of visual communication that enchanted my creative side especially within a spatial context due to the complexity of depicting geographical information.

Nonverbal communication is the most effective way to transcend information. I want to utilize design concepts to enhance user-experience for web development, cartography, and graphic design, as vehicle to transfer ideas and encourage discussion.
