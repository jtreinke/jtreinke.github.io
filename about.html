Welcome! My name is Jennifer Reinke and I am a recent Master of Geographic Information Science (GIS) with a design minor from the University of Minnesota - Twin Cities. I solve spatial problems, create maps, build websites, and am fixated with design and typography. I value the innovation that comes through collaboration with others as well as picking my brain on solo projects.

I developed GIS skills in various disciplines such as working as a Research Assistant at the John R. Borchert Map Library through U-Spatial, the university’s consortium for spatial research, where I worked on digital humanities’ projects, web development, and historical aerial photograph processing. Furthermore, I gained GIS experience while in the field with the Natural Resources Department for the City of Lakeville, as well as remote sensing techniques at the Polar Geospatial Center.

GIS applications first sparked my interest during my undergraduate degree as I majored in geography and French. I am fascinated by the interaction between culture, space, and place and my cartography lecture revealed these interactions through visual communication. I enjoyed the graphic challenges concerning geographical research, psychology, and design. Mapping continued to draw my attention as I lived and worked in Europe as navigating the winding streets with maps was often a factor of daily survival. Once again, my graduate courses enabled me to couple spatial data analysis in a visuallyp compelling way.

When I am not practicing GIS, coding, or design, I’m indulging in drawing, croquet, and wanderlust as a creative outlet. Follow me on LinkedIn, take a glimpse at my GitHub, or go to the contact page to reach out.
